Fashion Research part 1

How I explored the history and ethos behind my favorite unknown fashion brand

Existing before the era of fashion digitization, there are many brands whose collections, stories and influence have not been adequately preserved. This report details how I used various qualitative research methods, consolidating oral anecdotes and scattered online data, to learn more about one of the most influential French streetwear brands ever.

The first time I heard of Ron Orb was in 2020 through a friend who forwarded me an online listing for a 1990s denim jacket that he thought was misdated – in his words, the design looked "too ahead of its time." I got curious and looked the brand up, but couldn’t find any information besides a few other listings priced at hundreds of dollars each.


My research of the brand began with the development and deployment of an automated online marketplace alert bot in Python that could notify me via Discord in case new listings on platforms like Vinted and Depop matching the keywords “Ron Orb” were posted, eliminating the need to constantly check manually.


Being able to collect information from listings themselves, as well as reach out to the sellers who sometimes had more information about the brand, was instrumental in deepening my base understanding. Everything that I learned was recorded and organized on a spreadsheet and then marked as either assumption or fact (with confidence not certainty), constantly building upon previous entries.


I also collected data from vintage physical magazines that featured editorials and excerpts from the brand that I would scan and catalog. One of these magazines led me to a video produced by the brand that did not exist online, which I saved and released publicly to my fashion account on Instagram.


I was lucky enough to meet Ron himself after being introduced through a store owner in Paris who used to stock his collection. I visited his home and we shared a conversation full of insights, even recording an impromptu interview (from which I later translated and uploaded select segments to Instagram Stories). I feel a responsibility to share what I know about Ron Orb because I might be the only person in the world who both wants to and can, the designer himself preferring to focus on the future.


Being able to influence how such an important designer’s legacy is maintained, albeit among a small community of enthusiasts, has been an intensive and rewarding experience. While I consider Ron’s collections and talent to be irreplicable, I hope to start working with or adjacent to newer creatives that excite a similar passion for innovation, artistic expression and unique design.
